- Introduction -

Hi, my name is Jakub Folejtar and I am a 27 years old developer from Prague. I am a married man and proud father of 1.5 years old boy. To be a developer is not only a job for me, but rather a passion. I also like to take up on a challenge and enjoy being perfectionist about my work.


My interests towards programming started when I was around 12 years old. The first spark was set by my older brother who at that time was already full time coder, doing his magic in html and css and partially in Flash (R.I.P.). I picked up on those skills and tried to hone them, I was trying to code something all the time, and in my young mind, stupid ideas were flowing...


I was attending a general high school, with no specific direction set to continue afterwards. So at 15 y.o. I decided it would be best to switch to a more specific high school that specialises in programming. I started attending SPŠE Ječná and graduated there after 4 years. I did not really enjoy the school as the kind of stuff they were teaching were quickly getting obsolete and new technologies were coming out every now and then, so I had to catch up and learn on my own.


After graduating, I jumped straight into university (ČVUT FIT) to gain some more knowledge on programming, but after half a year I dropped out. The reason was that I am not the most patient person when it comes to things without direction, and this school made me feel like it first needs to teach me tons of stuff around programming before actually getting to programming. I continued on learning on my own, possibly missing on some interesting stuff, but I was master of my own time this way. I wanted to start getting more experience in the industry by working for some company, so I created a profile on job searching platform and tried to ask for a job. This resulted in my first real job experience in Socialbakers a.s.

- Career -

2014-2015 Socialbakers a.s. (Developer)

I started my career here as a junior support developer. My job was to handle or distribute technical requests coming to customer support. I was able to use this as a starting point of my learning curve.

2015-2015 E-commerce Holding a.s. (Developer)

Since there was a so called black Friday in Socialbakers due to lack of financial assets, I was fired as my junior position was not a necessity for the company. I searched for a new job, and for short period of time I settled in E-commerce Holding a.s. working on a project that should result in a framework able to create e-shops in minutes.

2015-2019 Socialbakers a.s. (Developer)

After some reconsideration from Socialbakers, they came up with offer to get back to the company, since I already spent quite some time learning the exact processes there, and was in a way a good employee, as I was eager to work quite fast and resolve lot of issues before they needed to be handled by other teams. This time, I was hired on 2 positions simultaneously.

One as the original support developer, the other as a data analyst for different needs across the whole company.


In 2017, after gathering some experience I joined different team, which was focused solely on programming, specifically grabbing data from social networks, mapping and enriching them to our needs, and saving them to different places as well per needs of the product. In this team I learned a lot about APIs, backend programming in PHP and after a migration, about Node-js. It was a great journey with a lot of challenges to overcome as each API behaves differently, releases new version often, has unexpected issues in the production run that needs to be resolved ASAP. In this team I was also doing maintenance duties overnight from time to time.

2019-2020 Socialbakers a.s. (Product Owner)

At this point I was offered a role as a Product Owner of the same team I was part of until now. The team was actually kind of company within company, so it made sense it had a 1:1 relation with it's Product owner. This was a really interesting experience which gave me insight from the other perspective, so I was able to pick up on some diplomatic skills and also was more able to see problems from different point of views. However, it was not meant for me as I was missing the rewarding feeling of completing a programming task, which you can have on almost daily basis as a programmer, so I decided to step out to be a developer again.

2020-now Socialbakers a.s. (Developer)

Now I am back in the team I was Product Owner for, so back in the old track, however with new views and great motivation to develop some great stuff.
